Research Program

Four Core Research Areas

My research program sits at the intersection of hydrology, earth system science, and artificial intelligence — addressing global water challenges from molecular to planetary scales.

01

Hydrological Modeling at Global Scale

Developing and applying large-scale hydrological models that capture the full complexity of the terrestrial water cycle, from precipitation to runoff, groundwater recharge, and evapotranspiration.

  • Coupling VIC, MOSART, and E3SM for coupled land-river simulations
  • Assimilating multi-source satellite observations (GRACE, MODIS, Sentinel)
  • Global-scale water budget analysis under climate projections
  • Urban water systems and potable water sustainability
VIC ModelMOSARTE3SMGRACE
02

AI / ML for Intelligent Water Systems

Building next-generation AI frameworks that combine physical constraints with deep learning to improve hydrological predictions and enable real-time, adaptive management of water resources.

  • Physics-informed neural networks for streamflow forecasting
  • Deep reinforcement learning for reservoir and dam operations
  • Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for drought prediction
  • Multi-model ensemble methods and uncertainty quantification
Deep LearningReinforcement LearningLSTM
03

Climate Resilience & Adaptation

Quantifying how climate change reshapes the frequency, intensity, and spatial distribution of hydrological extremes, and developing adaptation strategies for water-dependent communities and infrastructure.

  • Compound drought and heat event analysis under SSP scenarios
  • Flood risk assessment and urban stormwater management
  • Water-energy-food nexus under climate stress
  • Nature-based solutions for adaptive water infrastructure
DroughtFlood RiskClimate Adaptation
04

Transboundary Water Management

Applying AI-driven optimization and game-theoretic frameworks to navigate the political and technical complexities of shared river basins, supporting equitable and sustainable water allocation.

  • Multi-objective optimization of international reservoir systems
  • Game theory and Nash bargaining for water conflict resolution
  • Remote sensing for ungauged transboundary basins
  • Case studies: Nile, Mekong, Tigris-Euphrates, Amu Darya

How I Work

Research Approach

A convergent science approach that bridges computation, observation, and theory across spatial and temporal scales.

Earth Observation

Harnessing GRACE-FO, MODIS, Landsat, and Sentinel to constrain models with multi-scale observations of water storage, evapotranspiration, and land cover change.

Physics-Based Modeling

Running E3SM, VIC, and MOSART at regional-to-global scales on national supercomputing facilities (NERSC, Perlmutter) to simulate water cycle dynamics.

Machine Learning Integration

Embedding deep learning within physical model frameworks — using neural networks to learn residuals, emulate expensive computations, and improve predictions.

Decision Support

Translating scientific outputs into actionable frameworks — reservoir operating rules, early warning systems, and policy-relevant metrics for water managers.

Collaborative Science

Working across disciplinary boundaries with hydrologists, climate scientists, economists, and policy experts to tackle complex socio-environmental challenges.

Open Science

Committed to reproducible research — publishing code, data, and workflows on GitHub to advance transparency and accelerate scientific progress in the community.
